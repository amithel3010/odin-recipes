Vegeterian Tofu Based Bolognese

A signature dish at my house. There are many ways to make this recipe easier or shorter if you're feeling lazy, and it still turns out sick.

As a vegeterian, I am always looking for ways to diversfy my food. This recipe is great because it uses tofu in a way that is unusual for my family.

Ingredients

  • 500g dry pasta (1 pack, I use Rummo)
  • 300g tofu, crumbled (1 pack)
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halfed
  • 1-2 tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 1 medium yellow onion, roughly chopped
  • 1-2 carrots, peeled and finely chopped
  • 2 celery ribs, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, roasted
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 1-2 scallions
  • Some cilantro
  • Cumin, sweet and spicy paprika, chili flakes, sugar, slat and pepper
  1. Roast and cut red pepper:
    • Half and stem the pepper.
    • Preheat the oven broiler, and place the pepper halfs on a foil lined baking sheet.
    • They should be in the oven until they are blackend and blistered on all sides.
    • After taking them out of the oven, set them aside in a covered bowl for 10 minutes.
    • Use your hands to peel. If done correctly the peel should come off easily.
    • Cut however you want. I like cutting into medium sized cubes.
  2. Fry the crumbled tofu in a large, extremly hot pan with olive oil
      While frying add seasoning: salt, pepper, paprika, cumin and chili flakes.
  3. When the tofu gets to a texture reminiscent of fried minced meat, set it aside on a plate, on a paper towel to soak excess oil.
  4. In a large pan on low heat, add olive oil, the garlic slices and some chili flakes.
  5. when the oil starts bubbling (and before the garlic browns!) add the onions. keep on low heat.
  6. When the onion softenes (should take about 5-8 minutes) raise the heat to medium-high and add the carrots and the celery.
  7. when the carrots and celery soften, move to high heat and add the tomatoes and the roasted peppers.
    • Add salt, pepper and sugar directly on the tomatoes.
  8. After the tomatoes break apart and liquid pours out of them, cover the pan and move to medium-low heat.
  9. Now is when I usually start making the pasta. The tomatoes need to be on low heat for at least 15-20 minutes, and as long as there are enough liquids, the longer the better!
  10. When the pasta enters the water, Add the fried tofu to the tomatoes.
  11. Cook pasta until it is almost al dente, and then strain it and keep a cup of pasta water.
  12. Add some pasta water to the sauce, until you reach the desired consistency.
  13. Add pasta to sauce.
    • Add salt, pepper, and cumin directly to pasta.
  14. Garnish with scallions and cilantro.
  15. Enjoy!
